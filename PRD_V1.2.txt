**Version:** 1.2  
**Date:** February 18, 2026  
**Owner:** Product Team  
**Status:** Ready for Development

## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Product Vision
An accessible, institute‑scale education platform that enables students with disabilities (blind, deaf, hard of hearing, mute, ADHD, dyslexia, autism, cognitive, motor, etc.) to access educational content in formats that work for their specific needs—without being segregated into disability‑specific systems—while supporting complex multi‑level academic hierarchies (institutes, schools, departments, programs, years, divisions, sections) similar to large universities.  

### 1.2 The Problem
- **Fragmented experience for students with multiple disabilities.** They juggle separate tools for Braille, captions, simplified text, etc.  
- **High teacher burden.** Teachers manually create and manage multiple accessible versions of the same content.  
- **Coverage and compliance gaps.** Institutes struggle to prove WCAG 2.1 AA/AAA compliance and track which content is accessible in which formats.  
- **Structural complexity.** Large institutes have multiple colleges, programs, years, divisions, and elective structures that many tools either ignore or hard‑code per institute, limiting scalability.  

### 1.3 The Solution
A unified, multi‑tenant platform where:

- Students create **one accessibility profile** that activates relevant visual, auditory, cognitive, communication and navigation modules.  
- Teachers upload content **once**; an automatic conversion engine produces all required accessible formats (audio, captions/transcripts, Braille, simplified text, sign‑language placeholder).  
- Accessibility features **compose** cleanly (e.g., audio + captions + simplified + chunked view).  
- A configurable **hierarchy engine** models each institute’s structure and terms without code changes.  
- A **course & enrollment layer** models course catalog, term‑specific course offerings, sections/divisions and elective pools.  
- A **role & scoping layer** (RBAC + content targeting) ensures the right students and staff see the right materials and analytics, even with complex electives.  

### 1.4 Success Criteria
- **Accessibility Coverage:** 100% of published content available in all required formats within 5 minutes of upload (Tier‑1 formats).  
- **Teacher Efficiency:** 90% reduction in manual time spent on creating accessible content.  
- **User Adoption:** 50,000+ students and 100+ institutions within 12 months.  
- **Compliance:** WCAG 2.1 AA/AAA certification and audit‑ready reports.  
- **Student Outcomes:** 20%+ improvement in course completion rates for students with disabilities.  
- **Configurability:** 90%+ of new institutes onboarded without engineering changes.  
- **Scalability:** Support at least 50 concurrent institutes with distinct hierarchies and catalogs.

## 3. Core Domain Concepts

- **Institute Hierarchy:** Institute → School/College → Department → Program → Year → Division/Section.  
- **Term:** Configurable academic terms (semesters/trimesters) tied to academic years.  
- **Course:** Catalog entry (CS301 – Machine Learning) with metadata and prerequisites.  
- **Course Offering:** Course taught in a specific term and context, e.g., “CS301 – Machine Learning – Spring 2026 – Year 3 – Div A,B”; central object for enrollment, content, assessments, messaging, and analytics.  
- **Enrollment:** Student registered into a specific courseOffering and section for a term (including electives).  
- **Content Item:** A file or resource (PDF, video, audio, link, etc.) scoped to one or more courseOfferings/sections, with original file and converted accessible formats.  
- **Assessment:** A quiz/exam attached to a specific courseOffering.  
- **Accessibility Profile:** Per‑student configuration of disabilities and preferences, driving modules and defaults.

## 5. Feature Details

### 5.1 Unified Accessibility Profile System
**What:** One profile per student to declare disabilities, preferences, and devices; system activates relevant modules and default formats across all flows.  

**Key additions vs v1.1:**
- Profile drives **auto‑selection of formats per content item** and **auto‑applied extended time** in assessments.  
- Admin and teachers can see summary disability chips for students **only in their scope** (e.g., courseOffering they teach) and only at high level, with sensitive details gated by permissions.  

(Keep v1.1 requirements, acceptance criteria, and metrics.)

---

### 5.2 Automatic Content Conversion Engine
**What:** On teacher upload, system converts content to multiple accessible formats and surfaces progress, failures, and review queues.  

**Key clarifications:**
- Every conversion job is linked to both `contentId` and `courseOfferingId`, enabling teacher and admin views by course and term.  
- Tier‑1 conversions (audio, captions, transcripts, high‑contrast) must complete successfully before content is considered “Published” to students.  
- Tier‑2 conversions (Braille, Simplified, Sign Language, complex image descriptions) require teacher review in a dedicated **Conversion Queue** per teacher.  

(Keep detailed tiers, pipeline steps, and acceptance criteria from v1.1.)

---

### 5.3 Accessible Content Viewer
**What:** Unified viewer that lets students consume each content item in their preferred format, with smooth switching and strong keyboard/screen‑reader support.  

**Clarifications:**
- Viewer is always reached via a **courseOffering context**, from either the student Course page or New Content list.  
- Viewer header shows academic breadcrumb (Program, Year, Division, Course, Term) plus format selector.  
- Default format is chosen from the student’s profile; user can override per item (persisted as preference).  

(Keep detailed playback controls, chunking, progress tracking, and acceptance criteria.)

---

### 5.4 Multi‑Modal Assessment System
**What:** Assessments that accept text, file, audio, or video responses, enforce extended time, and remain fully accessible.  

**Clarifications:**
- Every assessment belongs to one `courseOfferingId`; students and teachers only see assessments for offerings where they are enrolled/assigned.  
- Assessment dashboard per course shows upcoming, in‑progress, and past assessments for that courseOffering.  

(Keep question types, accessibility features, acceptance criteria, and metrics.)

---

### 5.5 Accessible Communication Suite
**What:** Messaging, announcements, and (future) video calls with transcripts, captions, and multi‑channel notifications.  

**Clarifications:**
- Threads are always scoped: by courseOffering, by section, or direct user‑to‑user; role and scope determine visibility.  
- Messages and announcements can use the same content targeting engine as content items (institute → program → courseOffering → section → specific students).  

---

### 5.6 Teacher Content Management (Course‑Offering Centric)
**What:** Teacher dashboard for uploading, scoping, reviewing, and analyzing content per courseOffering.  

**Key requirements (consolidated + clarified):**
- Teachers land on a **Teaching Overview** showing courseOfferings they teach for the selected term, with counts for students, content items, and pending conversions.  
- Content upload flows:
  - Usually triggered from within a course’s Content tab, with courseOffering pre‑selected.  
  - If triggered from a global Content Library, teacher must select course, term, courseOffering and allowed sections; system validates RBAC.  
- The teacher can:
  - Publish one content item to multiple sections of the same courseOffering.  
  - See conversion status and formats per file.  
  - Access a per‑course **Content** tab with table of items and format status.  
  - Use a **Conversion Queue** tab to review Tier‑2 items.  

(Reuse detailed requirements, acceptance criteria, and metrics from v1.1, adjusting wording from “course” to “courseOffering” where necessary.)

---

### 5.7 Admin Dashboard & Analytics
**What:** Multi‑level dashboard for managing hierarchy, users, catalogs, enrollment and accessibility compliance.  

**Key clarifications:**
- Admins can inspect any term and view:
  - Students enrolled in each courseOffering, with disability chips and accessibility module usage.  
  - Teachers and the offerings/sections they teach, including schedules.  
  - Conversion success/failure rates by program/year/section and by format.  
- Dedicated **Conversions Monitor** lists failed/in‑progress jobs across the institute to coordinate remediation.  

(Keep v1.1 user stories, dashboards, reports, and metrics.)

### 5.8 Institute & Academic Hierarchy Management
Unchanged from v1.1, but explicitly used to drive breadcrumbs and filters on all dashboards.

### 5.9 Course Catalog, Offerings & Enrollment (incl. Electives)
**Key clarifications:**
- Student “My Courses This Term” is built strictly from `enrollments` + `courseOfferings` for the current term.  
- Elective selection UI is a dedicated screen under Courses → Enrollment, driven by `electiveGroups` and rules.  
- Core vs elective status is shown clearly on course cards.

Feature 10 and 11 from v1.1 remain; wording updated to emphasize `courseOfferingId` as the primary scope key for teachers, TAs, and students.

## 6. Non‑Functional Requirements

- Performance, security, privacy, auditability, and uptime targets remain as in PRD v1.1.  
- Special emphasis on:
  - Protecting disability data and accessibility profiles.  
  - Preventing cross‑program/section leakage of content and assessments.  

---

## 7. Risks & Mitigations

Same as v1.1 (AI conversion quality, scalability, teacher adoption, API costs, misconfigured hierarchy/enrollment), with an added mitigation:

- **Risk:** Incorrect courseOffering configuration or enrollment imports cause wrong content visibility.  
- **Mitigation:** Validation tools, simulation/preview mode for changes, strong audit logs, and dedicated admin training.

---

## 8. Out of Scope (v1.2 / MVP)

Unchanged from v1.1: AI tutoring, deep LMS/SSO integration beyond basic hooks, advanced gamification, full video‑conferencing suite, mobile apps (beyond responsive web), etc.
